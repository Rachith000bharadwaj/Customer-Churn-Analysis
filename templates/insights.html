Tableau Worksheets

1

Total Customers (KPI)

KPI Card

This worksheet displays the total number of unique customers in the Telco dataset as a Key Performance Indicator. It provides an immediate overview of the dataset scope. The customerID field is placed on the Text shelf with a COUNTD (Count Distinct) aggregation to ensure each customer is counted only once.

Field used: customerID → Text shelf
Aggregation: COUNTD (Count Distinct)
Chart type: Text / KPI card
Result: 7,043 total customers

Business Insight: The dataset contains 7,043 telecom customers. This number serves as the baseline for all percentage-based calculations and churn rate analysis across the entire dashboard.

2

Churn Rate (KPI)

Calculated Field

This worksheet calculates and displays the overall churn rate as a percentage. A calculated field is created to divide the number of churned customers by the total customer count. The Churn field is filtered to "Yes" to isolate churned customers.

Formula: SUM(IF [Churn]='Yes' THEN 1 ELSE 0 END) / COUNT([customerID])
Format: Displayed as Percentage
Filter: Churn = Yes
Result: ~26.5% churn rate

Business Insight: Approximately 1 in 4 customers has churned. A 26.5% churn rate is considered high in the telecom industry, indicating a significant retention problem that requires immediate attention and strategic intervention.

3

Churn by Gender

Bar Chart

This worksheet presents a grouped bar chart comparing churn across male and female customers. The gender field is placed on Columns, COUNT(customerID) on Rows, and Churn on the Color shelf to differentiate churned vs. non-churned customers visually.

Columns: gender
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Gender has minimal impact on churn behavior. Both male and female customers show nearly identical churn rates (~26-27%), suggesting that gender-based retention strategies would not be effective. The focus should shift to other factors.

4

Churn by Contract Type

Bar Chart

This worksheet analyzes how contract type influences churn. Customers are segmented into Month-to-Month, One-Year, and Two-Year contracts. The chart clearly shows that shorter commitments correlate with significantly higher churn rates.

Columns: Contract
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Month-to-month customers have the highest churn rate (~42%), while two-year contract customers show only ~3% churn. This is the strongest predictor of churn. Offering incentives to convert month-to-month customers to annual contracts would significantly reduce churn.

5

Churn by Internet Service

Bar Chart

This worksheet compares churn rates across three internet service categories: Fiber Optic, DSL, and No Internet Service. The analysis reveals a surprising pattern — customers with faster, more expensive internet service churn at a higher rate.

Columns: InternetService
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Fiber optic users have the highest churn rate (~42%) compared to DSL (~19%) and no internet (~7%). This suggests dissatisfaction with service quality or pricing. Despite offering premium speed, fiber optic customers may feel the service doesn't justify the cost.

6

Churn by Payment Method

Bar Chart

This worksheet examines the relationship between payment method and churn. Four payment methods are compared: Electronic Check, Mailed Check, Bank Transfer (Automatic), and Credit Card (Automatic). The results reveal that manual payment methods correlate with higher churn.

Columns: PaymentMethod
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Electronic check users have the highest churn rate (~45%), nearly double that of automatic payment users (~15-18%). Customers who set up automatic payments are more committed. Encouraging automatic payment enrollment could directly reduce churn.

7

Monthly Charges Distribution

Histogram

This worksheet creates a histogram to visualize the distribution of monthly charges across all customers. The MonthlyCharges field is dragged to create bins that group customers by their monthly spending levels.

Field: MonthlyCharges (binned)
Chart type: Histogram
Range: $18 to $118 per month
Avg churned: ~$74/month

Business Insight: Churned customers tend to have higher monthly charges (avg ~$74) compared to retained customers (avg ~$61). Customers paying more may have higher expectations. Consider loyalty discounts or premium service guarantees for high-paying customers to reduce churn risk.

8

Tenure Distribution

Histogram

This worksheet shows the distribution of customer tenure (how many months a customer has been with the company). The tenure field is binned to create a histogram that highlights the relationship between customer longevity and churn.

Field: tenure (binned)
Chart type: Histogram
Range: 1 to 72 months
Avg churned: ~18 months

Business Insight: Churned customers have a significantly shorter average tenure (~18 months) vs. retained customers (~38 months). Most churn happens in the first 12 months. Implementing a strong onboarding program and early engagement strategy within the first year is critical.

9

Churn by Senior Citizen

Bar Chart

This worksheet analyzes churn based on senior citizen status. The SeniorCitizen field (0 = Non-Senior, 1 = Senior) is placed on Columns with COUNT(customerID) on Rows and Churn on Color to compare both groups.

Columns: SeniorCitizen (0/1)
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Senior citizens have a higher churn rate (~41%) compared to non-seniors (~24%). Although seniors represent only ~16% of the dataset, their disproportionately high churn suggests they may need tailored support, simplified plans, or dedicated customer service.

10

Churn by Dependents

Bar Chart

This worksheet examines whether having dependents (children/family members) affects a customer's likelihood to churn. The Dependents field (Yes/No) is placed on Columns with churn-colored bars showing the retention pattern.

Columns: Dependents
Rows: COUNT(customerID)
Color: Churn (Red/Green)
Chart type: Grouped Bar Chart

Business Insight: Customers without dependents churn more (~31%) than those with dependents (~15%). Family plans create a stronger sense of commitment and switching cost. Promoting family bundles and multi-user discounts could significantly improve retention rates.

Key Findings & Conclusion

Based on the analysis of 7,043 telecom customers across 10 Tableau worksheets, the following actionable findings have been identified to reduce customer churn:

  • 1 The overall churn rate is ~26.5%, meaning roughly 1 in 4 customers leaves the service — a critical business concern.
  • 2 Gender has no significant impact on churn. Both male and female customers churn at nearly identical rates.
  • 3 Month-to-month contracts are the strongest churn predictor with ~42% churn rate vs. ~3% for two-year contracts.
  • 4 Fiber optic customers churn the most (~42%), possibly due to service quality dissatisfaction or pricing concerns.
  • 5 Electronic check payment users churn at ~45%, nearly double the rate of automatic payment users.
  • 6 Churned customers have higher monthly charges (avg $74) and shorter tenure (avg 18 months).
  • 7 Senior citizens are ~1.7x more likely to churn than non-senior customers.
  • 8 Customers without dependents churn at roughly double the rate of those with family members on the plan.
